Our Trip

Day 1: Hue﻿﻿﻿﻿ to A Luoi to Prao (165km)

Departing from Hue, our trip takes you along highway No 49 to the West of Thua Thien Hue Province. Going up and down mountains and passes, you can discover the great wild mountains, forests, falls, rivers and see the unique cultural lifestyle of minority group living along the Ho Chi Minh trail. It's great for photos and getting the "local" experiences.

We also stop at the Mau waterfalls to take a rest and enjoy the fresh, cool water.

We will have lunch at A Luoi town, then in the afternoon we travel on to Prao town and along this 100km you will discover the daily life of the Katu ethnic people.

Approx 5pm. we arrive at Prao town. You will have your own free time in the evening.

Day 2: Prao to Kham Duc (240km)

We spend this day on Ho Chi Minh trail. Along the road from Prao town to Kham Duc, you can enjoy the fresh air and feel the spirit of freedom travelling on the back of motorbikes with our friendly drivers. Stay a night in Kham Duc town.

Day 3: Prao to Kon Tum (240km)

After breakfast, we continue our trip along the Ho Chi Minh trail.

Admire the spectacular views of mountains and forests en route from Prao to Kon Tum and through the Central Highlands.

Ethnic minorities make up 51% of the population of Kontum. Most of them are Ba Na, Xo Dang, Gie Trieng, Gia Rai, B'Rau, and Ro Man. They work on fields in the mountains. We can meet them, visit their villages and also stop at some war memorials in the towns along the Ho Chi Minh Trail. In the late afternoon we arrive in Kon Tum City where we will spend the night.

Day 4: Kon Tum to Quang Ngai (194km)

This day we spend the morning visiting Kon Tum.

Kontum is in the Central Highlands. It shares the border with Laos, Cambodia on the west, Quang Nam Province on the north, Quang Ngai Province on the east and Gia Lai Province on the south.

Kontum has a colorful, diversified culture of ethnic groups. Their customs are unique with special traditional festivals like cong chieng (gongs), buffalo stabling, leaving- the- tomb, new rice festivals.

You will visit the Black Wooden Church, a Bana minority village with their Rong houses, meet an older Bahnar man who makes and plays his own instruments and maybe visit the Hill tribe museum. Tonight will be spent in Quang Ngai, arriving at the hotel in the late afternoon.

Day 5: Quang Ngai to Hoi An (105km)

The last day, we depart for My Son sanctuary, a World Heritage site since 1999. We stop there for you to explore the Champatemples, with their many architectural styles and sculptures representing every historical period of the Kingdom of Champa.

My Sonis considered one of the central temples of Hinduism in Southeast Asia and is the only legacy of this kind in Vietnam.
