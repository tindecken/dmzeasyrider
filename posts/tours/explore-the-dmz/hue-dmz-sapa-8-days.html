Our Trip

Day 1: Hue - DMZ -Cua Tung Beach

From Hue City, we take the Highway No.1 to Quang Tri town where you will explore the DMZ. Our first stop is Long Hung church, which still shows scars from the American war.

The next stop is QuangTri Old Citadel, a famous place, where fighting took place for 81 days and nights in 1972.

Then we ride along the coast from Cua Viet beach to Cua Tung beach passing a lot of beautiful and quiet coastline. Have lunch of local seafood and take a relaxing break in this area, with its wild and quiet beach.

In the afternoon, we come to Vinh Moc tunnels, the Demilitarised Zone and Doc Mieu military base. Then we come back to Cua Tung Beach and overnight there.

Day 2: Cua Tung Beach to Phong Nha Caves

Leaving the Cua Tung Beach, we ride to Phong Nha Caves. Check in at the hotel and after having lunch, you can take a boat trip to admire this famous Cave network.

PhongNha Caves are a complex of 14 grottoes situated in a region of Limestone Mountains in Ke Bang National Park.

Day 3: Phong Nha Caves to Tan Ky

Today we ride from Phong Nha to Tan Ky which is a district of Nghe An Province in the North Central Coastal region of Vietnam. You will have an amazing trip, passing tea plantations, rice fields, villages, mountains, valleys and waterfalls.

Tan Ky district is famous also for the star of theHo Chi Minh trail. Milestone number 0 is the starting point of this strategic transport route that supported the war in South Vietnam. Every day,thousands ofpeopletravel to see milestone 0,stoppingtoexplore, takepictures and recordmomentsstanding at themilestone. Approx 5:00 pm we arrive to Tan Ky district.

Day 4: Tan Ky to Cuc Phuong National Park

We take this day to visit Cuc Phuong Nation Park whichis located in Ninh Binh Province, in Vietnam's Red River Delta. Cuc Phuong was Vietnam's first national park and is the country's largest nature reserve. The park is one of the most important sites for biodiversity in Vietnam.

CucPhuong National Park is one of the most popular nature tourist nature destinations in Vietnam. Tens of thousands of Vietnamese and a steady stream of foreign tourists visit the park each year. Lodging and restaurant facilities are available at the park's entrance and within the park.

Day 5: Cuc Phuong National Park to Mai Chau

From Cuc Phuong National Park, we ride to Mai Chau, a chain of villages surrounded by a lush green valley with stilt houses bordering both sides of the roads.

MaiChau Valley, immerse yourselves in the romantic scenery of green mountains, the beauty of simple houses on stilts and the villages of the White Thai ethnic minority.

Tonight you will have a "local" experience, staying in a traditional Thai stilt house and you may have a chance to attend a Thai folk show.

Day 6: Mai Chau to Son La

We continue our motorbike trip from Mai Chau to Son La Province.

The province is populated by various ethnic groups, including the Ma, H'mong, Dao, Muong, Kinh, Khmer, Tay, Thai, and so on. On route you can feel the fresh air, seemajestic mountains, the daily life of the ethnic minorities in their hamlets, tea-growing farms or blossoming ban flowers, which color the mountains white. In particular, you can explore the unique cultural values ​​ofthe peoples in the Northwest.

In the afternoon we arrive at Son La town. Check into the hotel and spend a night there. In the evening you can enjoy many specialties of Thai ethnic.

Day 7: Son La to Dien Bien Phu

Today, our destination is Dien Bien Phu. This trip enables you to enjoy the beautiful scenery and popular hill town of Dien Bien close to the Laos border.

We spend the day around Dien Bien Phu visiting former battlefields. It was here that the French planned to lure the Viet Minh, led by Ho Chi Minh, into the valley by sending 16,000 French troops as a diversion The French however did not count on the brilliant leadership of General Vo Nguyen Giap who somehow managed to place the Vietnamese artillery on the surrounding ridges. From March until May 1954 the Viet Minh pounded the French positions until the French capitulated with the loss of 3,000 men. It's estimated that the Viet Minh lost more than twice that number. Later you visit the head quarters of General Giap during the Dien Bien Phu campaign. We will have the chance to meet several of the different ethnic minority tribes here, when we visit the villages of both the Thai and H'mong people.

Day 8: Dien Bien Phu to Sapa

Welcome to our last destination, Sapa- the cityin the mist. It is exactlyas the namesays, as nature hasendowedthisregiontravel withverydistinctfeaturesthatfewplacescan offer. Everymorningwhenyou wake upat dawn, SaPaisimmersedin foglayers, that are dense and cover everything in white, quiet space - a poetic landscape that is also flowing with romance. At 1600m from sea level its year-round temperate climate is cool.

Sapahas become a tourist destination, ideal for domestic travelers and international tourists. It will leave you with unforgettable impressions, during the Hmong market-place on Saturday evening and as darkness begins falling, romantic lyrics of couples ring out, like gold into the night, endless ...
