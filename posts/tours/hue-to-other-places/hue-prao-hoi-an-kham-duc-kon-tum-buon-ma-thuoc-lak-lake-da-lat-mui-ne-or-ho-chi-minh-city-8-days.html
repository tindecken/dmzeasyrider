Our Trip

Day 1: Hue﻿﻿﻿﻿ to A Luoi to Prao (165km)

Depart from Hue City, our trip takes you along highway No 49 to the West of Thua Thien Hue Province. Going up and down mountains and passes, you can discover the great wild mountains, forests, falls, rivers and see the unique cultural lifestyle of minority group living along the Ho Chi Minh trail. That's great for photos and getting the "local" experience.

We also stop at Mau waterfalls to take a rest and enjoy the fresh, cool water.

We will have lunch at A Luoi town and then in the afternoon we travel on to Prao town. During this 100km you will discover the daily life of the Katu ethnic people. Approx 5pm. we arrive to Prao town. Free time at night.

Day 2: Prao - Hoi An

Our trip will continue with riding along the Ho Chi Minh trail again from Prao to Da Nang. Enjoy the fresh air and feel the spirit of freedom which riding on the back of motorbikes with our friendly drivers.

Along the way we stop at the KaTu ethnic minority village.

Travelling through Danang we stop at China Beach and Marble Mountain. We arrive in Hoi An in the early afternoon, giving you enough free time to shop and explore Hoi An Town.

Day 3: Hoi An to Kham Duc (150km)

After breakfast, we depart from Hoi An to My Son sanctuary, a World Heritage site since 1999. We stop there for you to explore the Champatemples with their many architectural styles and sculptures representing every historical period of the Kingdom of Champa.

We continue our trip by crossing the river by boat to Thanh My, where we stop for lunch. In the afternoon, we travel along the Ho Chi Minh Trail to Kham Duc. Along the way we stop at a beautiful waterfall to take photos.

Day 4: Kham Duc to Kon Tum (220km)

On the road to Kon Tum we will stop at Dakga waterfall where you can swim in the cool water. Our next stops are the hill tribe villages, Dak To-Tan Canh and the victory monument of VC in 1972.

In the afternoon we arrive in Kon Tum.

You will visit the Black Wooden Church, a Bana minority village with their Rong houses, meet an older Bahnar man who makes and plays his own instruments and maybe visit the Hill tribe museum.

Day 5: Kon Tum to Buon Me Thuot (240km)

To go to Buon Me Thuot, we will pass by Pleiku City in the central highlands province of Gia Lai. This city is famous for its magnificent waterfalls, green forests, rugged mountains and charming lakes, of which Bien Ho (Sea lake) is famous. We will stop there for a while.

This is also the land of rubber tree and coffee plantations.

Arriving at Buon Me Thuot, you can take a break and enjoy a cup of coffee, because this land is famous with making the Best Coffee in Vietnam.

Day 6: Buon Ma Thuot to Lak Lake (150km)

Today, the majestic Dray Sap waterfalls and picturesque Lak lake are our destination.

In the afternoon, check into the hotel, then we visit the local Mnong Ethnic hill tribe.

Arriving at this Central Highland, you will be able to taste specialities such as grilled meat, bamboo-tube rice and ruou can (wine drunk out of a jar through pipes).

Day 7: Lak Lake to Dalat (170km)

It's really wonderful to view the everyday activities of the Mo-nong ethenic group's villages, by taking a ride on an elephant. Therefore, in the morning you will have free time to ride an elephant amongst the villages or discover scenic Lak lake in a traditional dugout canoe or by riding an elephant also.

We spend the rest of the day enjoying the beautiful landscape along the road to Dalat, arriving in the late afternoon.

Overnight in Da Lat City, free time to walk around the City at night.

Day 8: Dalat to Mui Ne or Ho Chi Minh City

The morning we will take you to visit some of most notable destinations of this romantic city. Then we ride to Mui Ne or Ho Chi Minh City, our last destination of this tour.

Thank you so much for taking 8 days with our motorbike tour from the Central to the South. We hope that you have had an unforgettable time to discover a real Vietnam by motorbike.
